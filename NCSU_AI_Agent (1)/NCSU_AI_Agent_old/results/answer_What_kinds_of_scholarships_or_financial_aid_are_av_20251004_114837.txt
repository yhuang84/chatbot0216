## 1. Undergraduate Scholarships and Financial Aid

### A. Types of Scholarships
The Wilson College of Textiles offers a variety of scholarships primarily for undergraduate students, including both merit-based and need-based awards. These scholarships aim to recognize academic excellence, leadership, service, and financial need.

- **Merit-Based Scholarships:** Awarded based on academic success, leadership, community involvement, writing ability, and motivation in the textile industry.
- **Need-Based Scholarships:** Awarded based on financial need as determined by the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).

### B. Major Scholarship Programs for Incoming First-Year Students

The College provides several prestigious scholarships for incoming first-year students, many of which are renewable for four years:

| Scholarship Name                  | Amount (Annual)       | Enrichment Grants       | Eligibility Highlights                                         | Merit/Need-Based     |

| **ITT/Roger Milliken Centennial** | $22,000              | Up to $7,500           | Must major in Polymer and Color Chemistry, Textile Engineering, or Textile Technology | Merit               |
| **Centennial Scholarship**       | $20,000              | Up to $7,500           | Open to all Textiles majors                                   | Merit               |

- **Enrichment Grants:** Many scholarships include enrichment grants that can be used for study abroad, career development, internships, and other educational opportunities.
- **Scholar Programming:** Recipients of Centennial and Textile Pioneer Scholarships participate in specialized programming that includes leadership development, career readiness courses (e.g., T 104 Fabric of Success and Career Readiness), and honors seminars (T 491H) taught by the Dean and faculty.

### C. Application Process and Eligibility
- Students must apply for undergraduate admission to NC State by **November 10** (Early Action deadline) and select a Textiles major to be endorsed for the scholarship application.
- The scholarship application opens in early to mid-December via **PackAssist** (https://ncsu.academicworks.com/).
- For need-based scholarships, students must complete the **FAFSA** by January 31 of the enrollment year.
- The Textile Pioneer Scholarship requires residency in qualifying rural or non-metropolitan counties in North Carolina.
- All first-year scholarships are merit-based, with some also requiring demonstrated financial need.

### D. Additional Scholarship Opportunities
- The College supports external scholarships and encourages students to explore other funding sources.
- Study abroad scholarships are available, including those through the Wilson College and NC State Study Abroad Office.
- Students can apply financial aid and scholarships to study abroad programs if courses fulfill degree requirements.

---

## 2. Transfer Student Scholarships

- The Wilson College of Textiles also offers scholarships specifically for transfer students.
- These scholarships are merit-based and require the student to choose a Textiles major.
- Transfer scholarship applications and details are available on the College’s scholarship webpage.

---

## 3. Graduate Scholarships and Financial Aid

### A. Assistantships and Fellowships
Graduate students in the Wilson College of Textiles, particularly in the Textile Engineering, Chemistry and Science (TECS) department, have access to several forms of financial support:

- **Graduate Research Assistantships (RA):** Funded by faculty research grants, these provide tuition coverage, health insurance, and a stipend. Students work on research projects aligned with their thesis or dissertation.
- **Graduate Teaching Assistantships (TA):** Students assist in teaching courses or labs and receive tuition coverage, health insurance, and a stipend.
- **Fellowships:** Competitive awards that provide financial support without work requirements, often viewed favorably by employers. Examples include:
  - National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship
  - NIH Research Service Awards
  - EPA Science to Achieve Results Fellowship
  - Ford Foundation Fellowships
  - Department of Defense SMART Scholarship
  - Sigma Xi Grants in Aid of Research

### B. Graduate Student Support Plan (GSSP)
- Provides tuition remission and health insurance benefits for eligible graduate students with assistantships or fellowships.
- Eligibility requires full-time enrollment and satisfactory academic progress.

### C. Other Financial Support
- Alumni Association Graduate Fellowships and Andrews Fellowships for first-year graduate students.
- Travel grants for conference attendance (limited funds, require presentation).
- Residency requirements and tuition support policies apply, with guidance provided to establish in-state residency for tuition purposes.

---

## 4. Military and Veteran Scholarships

- The College of Textiles participates in NC State’s military-affiliated scholarship programs.
- Examples include:
  - **Textile Military Scholarship:** For College of Textiles students in sophomore, junior, or senior years who are current ROTC members.
  - **Libertees Scholarship:** Preference for veterans or children of veterans in the College of Textiles.
- Students must apply through **PackAssist** by the priority deadline (usually February 15).
- Additional military-related scholarships and grants are available through NC State’s Military and Veteran Services office.

---

## 5. Summary of Financial Aid Resources

- **Merit-based scholarships** for incoming first-year and transfer students, with significant awards and enrichment grants.
- **Need-based scholarships** requiring FAFSA completion.
- **Graduate assistantships and fellowships** providing tuition, health insurance, and stipends.
- **Study abroad scholarships and financial aid** applicable to international education experiences.
- **Military and veteran scholarships** tailored for service members, veterans, and their families.
- **Additional external scholarships** and funding opportunities encouraged.
